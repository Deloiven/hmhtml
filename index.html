Swans are large waterfowl belonging to the "duck" family and the "goose-like" order. There are only seven varieties of such birds left on Earth, with 10 species extinct. It turns out that there were about 17 species, some of which disappeared and, of course, not without human involvement. Swans are also distinguished by the fact that they have a unique plumage color, consisting of white, black and gray shades.
